## QUT Academic Integrity Policy (MOPP C/5.3)

### Scope

Applies to all undergraduate and postgraduate coursework students. Research higher degree students follow the QUT Code for Responsible Conduct of Research instead.

### Four Foundational Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Educative | Support students in developing integrity-based approaches through training and resources |
| Evidenced | Link curriculum and assessment with academic integrity; require verified identity assessments |
| Equitable | Clearly define violations and provide transparent investigation processes |
| Everyone Responsible | All community members share accountability for maintaining standards |

### What Constitutes a Breach

A breach is any conduct - intentional or unintentional - that fails to demonstrate ethics, fairness, honesty, respect, and accountability in academic work.

Major categories:

| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| Cheating | Unauthorized materials, communication, or assistance during examinations |
| Collusion | Unauthorized collaboration on assessable work |
| Contract cheating | Outsourcing solutions to a third party, whether for payment or not |
| Fabrication | Creating fictitious information, data, or sources |
| Falsification | Misrepresenting or altering information |
| Impersonation | Having someone else complete assessment |
| Plagiarism | Representing others' work as one's own |
| Self-plagiarism | Reusing previously-assessed own work without authorization |
| Unauthorized technology use | Using tools that "replace or obscure the student's own academic effort" |

### Breach Classification and Consequences

**Poor Academic Practice** - minor, typically unintentional violations:
- Formal recorded finding
- Warning issued
- Educative requirements (additional training, resubmission, or alternative assessment)
- No mark penalty

**Academic Misconduct** - serious, intentional, or repeated violations:
- Managed under the Management of Student Misconduct Policy (MOPP E/8.1)
- May result in formal penalties

## Generative AI (GenAI) Policy

QUT's Academic Integrity Policy explicitly addresses AI use in assessment:

- Using AI tools in assessment **without authorization** is a breach: "using artificial intelligence tools in assessment unless instructed to do so."
- Submitting work "produced or modified, wholly or in part, by an artificial intelligence tool where such actions are not authorised in the assessment task" is a breach.
- Using tools that "generate, modify, translate, paraphrase or otherwise alter assessment responses" without authorization constitutes unauthorized technology use.
- Unit coordinators specify whether and how GenAI may be used for each assessment task.
- AI-generated content is **not a credible academic source** and must not be cited as one.

QUT's critical evaluation framework for any GenAI use:
- **Accuracy**: GenAI can produce plausible-sounding but unsourced content
- **Bias**: May stem from training data or creator bias
- **Privacy**: Tools may collect and store user data - review terms before use
- **Creatorship**: Unknown training data origins make output unreliable as an academic source

## QUT Student Code of Conduct

Applies to all enrolled students across all activities - on-campus, off-campus (if affecting the university), and online.

Key obligations relevant to this project:
- Act with integrity and honesty
- Use ICT resources appropriately
- Comply with university policies and course requirements
- Provide accurate information regarding enrollment and funding matters
- Not engage in conduct that "damages, or is reasonably likely to damage, the reputation of the University"

Prohibited conduct includes false or fraudulent representations, unauthorized access to confidential information, and misuse of ICT resources.

## Relevance to This Project

The Believer project is a QUTAS student club project with university (QUT EER) funding. It is not a formal academic submission, but several intersections with QUT academic integrity policy apply:

### AI-Assisted Documentation

Claude Code (Anthropic) is used openly and extensively to assist with writing and maintaining project documentation in this repo. This is an acknowledged working practice, not a concealed use of AI.

- All AI-generated or AI-assisted content in this repo is project documentation (engineering records, logs, parameter files) - not academic assessment work.
- The technical facts, decisions, and engineering judgments in these documents originate with the project team (Julian Williams, Charlotte Kelly, QUTAS). Claude assists with formatting, consistency, and writing quality.
- If any materials from this repo are used in formal academic work (reports, theses, presentations), QUT's GenAI policy applies to that submission: the use of AI in producing the submitted work must comply with the specific unit's authorization.

### Funding Accountability

The project received $689.50 in EER School club activity funding (approved 2026-05-20/21). Accurate record-keeping of expenditure - maintained in `docs/project/purchase-history/purchase-history.md` - is part of responsible use of university funds and consistent with the Code of Conduct obligation to "provide accurate information regarding enrollment matters" (extended here to funding matters).

### BVLOS Operations and Regulatory Compliance

Operating a drone BVLOS requires compliance with CASA regulations and engagement with QUT's Chief Remote Pilot. The Code of Conduct requires students to "comply with health and safety requirements" and not breach Commonwealth or Queensland laws. This project's regulatory pathway is documented in `docs/project/project-overview.md` and `docs/operations/manual.md`.
